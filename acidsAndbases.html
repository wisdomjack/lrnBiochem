Acids and Bases

Historically humans came up with 3 definitions definitions of acids and bases. In biochemistry we work with Brønsted–Lowry definition. In the Brønsted–Lowry theory acids and bases are defined by the way they react with each other: an acid donates a proton and a base accepts a proton. The species then form conjugate pairs

Acids and bases can be divide into strong and weak. You will encounter problems that will involve strong and weak acids and bases. Both of these types of problems will have its own strategies and shortcuts, therefore it is important that you can identify what type of acid or base you are dealing with.



Strong acids and bases

Strong acid such as HCl, HNO3 and H2SO4 dissociate completely in aqueous solution (water) into H+ and conjugate weak base Cl-, NO3-, SO4- respectively. Yes, strong acids have weak conjugate bases, it is important to keep in mind that all chemical process at least in theory are reversible
